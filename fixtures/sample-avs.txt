AFTER VISIT SUMMARY — Emergency Department
Date: 04/18/2026

Reason for visit: Cellulitis, right lower leg

You were diagnosed with a skin infection (cellulitis). You are being discharged with oral antibiotics.

MEDICATIONS
- Amoxicillin 500 mg by mouth twice daily (approximately every 12 hours) for 7 days. Take with food.
- Acetaminophen 500 mg every 6 hours as needed for pain or fever.

FOLLOW-UP
- Schedule a follow-up visit with your primary care provider within 2–3 days, or sooner if not improving.
- Return to the ER or seek urgent care if you develop fever above 101.5°F, rapidly spreading redness, severe pain, or confusion.

ACTIVITY
- Elevate the leg when resting.
- Avoid prolonged standing today. Short walks are OK if comfortable.
- Do not lift heavy objects over 10 lbs for 3 days.

DIET
- Drink plenty of fluids.
- Avoid alcohol while on antibiotics.

WHEN TO SEEK CARE
- Call 911 for trouble breathing, chest pain, severe confusion, or fainting.
- Go to urgent care or ER for fever over 101.5°F, red streaks moving up the leg, or worsening pain/swelling despite medications.
